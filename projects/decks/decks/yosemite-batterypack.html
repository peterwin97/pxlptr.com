Field Report 001 — Who We Arepg. 02

Four people who'd rather be cold and far from cell signal

The first page of this notebook — who put it together, and why.

Focus Finder Media is a four-person field production crew. We don't shoot from hotels. We carry our kit in on foot, sleep where we're filming, and build every story around the conditions we actually find — not the ones we plan for.

We started doing this because the gear-review and travel-influencer space felt staged. We wanted to make films that looked and felt like the trip actually went — including the parts that almost didn't work.

Why We Exist

To document remote places and the equipment that makes working in them possible — honestly, on camera, as it happens.

Field Note #001

"I started filming trips because I kept coming home and not being able to explain what happened out there. Not the views — the waiting, the cold mornings, the gear failing at the worst time and somehow still getting the shot. That's the part people actually want to see. Every trip since has been an attempt to get closer to that."

Personal Mission

Make the remote feel reachable — and make sure the equipment in frame is equipment that actually held up.
